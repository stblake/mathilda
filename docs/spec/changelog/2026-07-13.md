# Changelog: week of 2026-07-13 (Mon) вҖ“ 2026-07-19 (Sun)

Feature additions and fixes recorded during this week.

## RootReduce threads over polynomial / rational-function coefficients (2026-07-17)

`RootReduce` now canonicalises the constant-algebraic **coefficients** of a
polynomial or rational function in a free variable, not just standalone constant
algebraic numbers and variable-radicand towers. Previously
`RootReduce[(Sqrt[2] + Sqrt[3] - Sqrt[5 + 2 Sqrt[6]]) x^2 + x + 1]` was returned
unchanged because the whole expression carries the free symbol `x`, so the
`qqbar` constant-number engine declined it and the parametric-tower engine (which
handles radicands that *carry* a free variable) did not apply.

- **New recursive coefficient threading** (`rr_thread_coeffs` in
  `src/rootreduce.c`). After the constant-number (`flint_qqbar_canonical`) and
  parametric-tower (`flint_algebraic_field_canonical`) engines both decline, the
  expression is walked structurally and each **maximal constant-algebraic
  subexpression** is canonicalised via `qqbar`, with the free-variable structure
  left intact. The rebuilt tree is evaluated once so a vanishing coefficient
  collapses its monomial (`Times[0, x^2] вҶ’ 0`, dropped by `Plus`).
- **Identity-preserving.** `flint_qqbar_canonical` returns `NULL` immediately for
  anything carrying a free symbol, so the walk is cheap; when no coefficient
  changes the rebuilt tree is `expr_eq` to the input and the original is returned
  unchanged. Plain polynomial cancellation is still *not* performed (that remains
  `Cancel`'s job): `RootReduce[(x^2-1)/(x-1)]` is unchanged.
- Examples now closed: `(Sqrt[2]+Sqrt[3]-Sqrt[5+2Sqrt[6]]) x^2 + x + 1 вҶ’ 1 + x`;
  `a x^2 + Sqrt[8] x вҶ’ 2 Sqrt[2] x + a x^2`; `Sqrt[8]/x вҶ’ 2 Sqrt[2]/x`. New
  regression cases in `tests/test_rootreduce.c`; memory-clean under valgrind.

## Cherry stress-test gap fixes вҖ” robust complex constants + constant-offset exponents (2026-07-17)

A broad stress campaign (176-case soundness sweep + a new permanent `cherry_stress_tests`
suite) found **zero soundness bugs** but surfaced completeness gaps; two are now closed
(`cherry_ei.c`).

- **A1 complex constants вҖ” now robust across `Q(iвҲҡd)`.** The lone complex-conjugate ei
  pair previously closed only for the fields Mathilda's `Solve` happened to crack
  (`E^x/(xВІ+2x+3)` over `Q(iвҲҡ2)` declined). Root cause: `Together` does not cancel the two
  complex-linear factors `(x+a_i)` back against the real quadratic, so the residual is
  over-determined and `Solve` declines. New fallback `rt_cherry_ei_conjpair_nf` solves the
  system **over Q** by the number-field basis method: write `a = center Вұ chs` (chs the
  algebraic generator, `chsВІ = disc` a negative rational), carry `c0 = cza + czbВ·chs`,
  `c1 = cza вҲ’ czbВ·chs` and `y` with rational unknowns, reduce the residual mod `chsВІ вҲ’ disc`,
  split into the `{1, chs}` Q-basis, and `Solve` over Q. Fires ONLY when the direct solve
  failed, so existing closures stay byte-identical. Now closes `E^x/(xВІ+2x+3)`,
  `E^x/(xВІ+2x+7)`, `E^x/(xВІвҲ’2x+3)`, `E^x/(xВІ+4x+7)`, `(3x+1)E^x/(xВІ+2x+3)`, вҖҰ over any
  `Q(iвҲҡd)` (diff-back exact). (`center` uses `Simplify` вҖ” `Together` leaves the conjugate
  sum unreduced.)
- **Constant exponent offset.** `E^(c + h(x))` with `c` an x-free constant (e.g.
  `E^(1/x+2) = EВІВ·E^(1/x)`) now closes: the constant is factored out (E^c folds into the
  cofactor) before the P2 recognition, which the inflated `deg(p)` had defeated. Fires only
  when the polynomial part of the exponent is a nonzero constant, so genuine polynomial
  exponents (`E^(x+1)`, `E^((xвҒҙ+a)/xВІ)`) are untouched.
- **Deferred (not user-facing):** the non-monic dilog kernel `Log[2x+1]/(x+1)` still declines
  on the `RischTranscendental` (cherry_dilog) path, but the FULL `Integrate` cascade closes
  it вҖ” so users are unaffected. Closing it inside cherry_dilog needs rational-root interpolant
  support (a separate effort); `Log[x+1/2]/(x+1)` declines everywhere and tracks the same gap.

## Exponent вҖ” polynomial exponent extraction (2026-07-17)

New builtin `Exponent[expr, form]` / `Exponent[expr, form, h]` (`src/poly/exponent.c`),
giving the maximum power with which `form` appears in the expanded form of `expr`.

- Default aggregator `h = Max`; `Min` gives the lowest power and `List` the sorted,
  de-duplicated exponent set. `Exponent[0, x]` is `-Infinity` (empty set, `Max[] = -Infinity`).
- `form` may be a symbol, a kernel (`Sin[x]`, `Log[x]`), or a product of terms (the exponent of
  a product is the largest `k` with `form^k` dividing each monomial).
- Rational and symbolic exponents are supported: `Exponent[x^(n+1) + 2 Sqrt[x] + 1, x] =
  Max[1/2, 1 + n]`. Laurent (negative) exponents too: `Exponent[1/x, x] = -1`.
- Purely syntactic вҖ” `expr` is expanded first but no zero-coefficient recognition is attempted.
- Attributes `Listable, Protected`; `Listable` makes `Exponent[expr, {f1, f2, вҖҰ}]` thread into
  the per-form list of exponents. Argument errors emit `Exponent::argt` / `Exponent::nonopt` and
  return the call unevaluated.
- Docstring in `info.c`; symbol interned in `sym_names.c`; tests in `tests/test_exponent.c`
  (13 groups, valgrind-clean).

## Integrate вҖ” Cherry dilogarithm: transcendental-constant root spacing (2026-07-16)

`Integrate` now closes dilogarithm integrands whose linear-factor roots have spacing
`вү  1`, the transcendental-constant case previously deferred by `cherry_dilog.c`:

- `вҲ« Log[2+x]/x dx = Log[2] Log[x] вҲ’ PolyLog[2, вҲ’x/2]`
- `вҲ« Log[2x+3]/(xвҲ’1) dx` (and the general `Log[x+a]/(xвҲ’b)`, monic-linear kernels).

Mechanism (all diff-back gated, so a mis-generation can only decline):
- A degree-1 ОЈ-decomposition dilog `PolyLog[2, g]` whose derivative leaves a `Log` of a
  **positive constant** (e.g. `D[PolyLog[2,вҲ’x/2]] = вҲ’Log[1+x/2]/x` contributes a `Log[2]`)
  is now admitted; the constant flows into a real `Log[2] Log[x]` answer term. A residual
  `Log` of an **x-dependent** argument or of a **non-positive/complex** constant (the
  `iПҖ` tell-tale of a reversed root pair) still drops the candidate.
- Candidate derivatives are normalized `Log[g] вҶ’ PowerExpand[Log[Factor[g]]]` so a scaled
  linear factor `Log[1+x/2]` splits into `Log[1/2] + Log[2+x]`, exposing the monic kernel.
- The coefficient solve moved from `SolveAlways` to an explicit
  `Solve[Thread[Flatten[CoefficientList[В·,{u,x}]] == 0], {unknowns}]` so a transcendental
  constant `Log[c]` in the coefficients stays a constant instead of being solved for.

Also folded in two stale strict-decline regression tests that the previously-landed
complex-quadratic Ei / Li layer already closes correctly (diff-back verified):
`вҲ« E^x/(xВІ+1) dx` (conjugate `ExpIntegralEi` pair) and `вҲ« 1/(Log[x]ВІ+1) dx` (conjugate
`LogIntegral` pair over `Q(i)`).

## Performance вҖ” Plus/Times like-term collection is O(n) (2026-07-16)

`builtin_plus` and `builtin_times` collected like terms (same base вҶ’ summed
coefficients / summed exponents) with a linear scan of every existing group,
making a sum or product of *n* terms over *g* distinct bases O(nВ·g) вҖ” quadratic
when the terms don't collapse. Both now index groups in an open-addressing hash
table keyed on `expr_hash(base)`, so collection is O(1) expected per term. The
canonical output form is unchanged (group encounter order preserved; the
evaluator's Orderless pass still canonicalises the result), so results are
byte-for-byte identical to before вҖ” only faster.

Measured (Apple M-series):

- `Total[cВ·x^k]` over 10вҒө monomials (10Ві distinct powers): **1.65 s вҶ’ 0.50 s**.
- Product of 4В·10вҒҙ distinct symbolic factors: **41 s вҶ’ 0.23 s** (was O(nВІ)).

Both operations now scale linearly in the number of terms. Touches
`src/plus.c` and `src/times.c` only.

## Special functions вҖ” SinhIntegral and CoshIntegral (2026-07-16)

Added the hyperbolic sine and cosine integrals, the imaginary-axis siblings of
`SinIntegral` / `CosIntegral`:

- **`SinhIntegral[z]`** вҖ” Shi(z) = вҲ«вӮҖб¶» Sinh[t]/t dt. Entire and odd, no branch
  cut (Shi(z) = вҲ’i Si(i z)). New in `src/special_functions/sinhintegral.c`.
- **`CoshIntegral[z]`** вҖ” Chi(z) = EulerGamma + Log[z] + вҲ«вӮҖб¶» (Cosh[t] вҲ’ 1)/t dt.
  Logarithmic singularity at 0, branch cut on `(вҲ’вҲһ, 0]` (Chi(z) = Ci(i z) вҲ’ i ПҖ/2).
  New in `src/special_functions/coshintegral.c`.

Each is `Listable, NumericFunction, Protected` with an Information string.
Highlights:

- **Numeric evaluation** for machine- and arbitrary-precision reals and
  complexes via MPFR / the `ncpx` toolkit. The convergent Maclaurin series are
  the trigonometric ones with the alternating sign removed (all terms positive),
  so the real paths carry no cancellation guard; large |z| uses the
  optimally-truncated asymptotic (`cosh F + sinh G` for Shi, `sinh F + cosh G`
  for Chi) with a Stokes constant off the real axis and the principal `Log`
  branch for Chi. Reals whose result overflows a C double (e.g.
  `SinhIntegral[10.^6] вүҲ 1.5166Г—10^434288`) return an extended-exponent MPFR
  real rather than `Infinity`.

  Negative real `Chi[-x]` returns the from-above branch value `Chi(|x|) + IПҖ`.
- **Derivatives** (`src/calculus/deriv.c`): `D[SinhIntegral[z],z] = Sinh[z]/z`,
  `D[CoshIntegral[z],z] = Cosh[z]/z` (with chain rule).

  `Integrate[Cosh[a x]/x, x] = CoshIntegral[a x]`. The trigonometric base cases

## Parser вҖ” top-level newline terminates a statement (2026-07-16)

Fixes [GitHub issue #20](https://github.com/stblake/mathilda/issues/20): a file
loaded with `Get["file.m"]` whose expressions were on separate lines *without*
trailing `;` returned the wrong value. Two consecutive top-level expressions
such as

```
Series[Sin[x], {x, 0, 5}]
Integrate[Sin[x], x]
```

were being joined by **implicit multiplication** (`Series[вҖҰ] * Integrate[вҖҰ]`)
because the parser treated the line break as ordinary whitespace, so `Get`
returned the product instead of the last statement's value.

The parser now treats a line break at **bracket depth 0** as a soft statement
terminator (Mathematica semantics), while line breaks *inside* `()`, `[]`, `{}`,
`<|вҖҰ|>`, or `[[вҖҰ]]` remain insignificant. Concretely:

- `a\nb` вҶ’ two statements `a`, `b` (was `a b`).
- `f[a,\n b]` and `{1,\n 2}` вҶ’ still one expression each.
- A line ending in an operator (`1 +\n2`, `x =\n 5`) still continues onto the
  next line.

Implementation (`src/parse.c`): `ParserState` gains a `bracket_depth` counter
(maintained by the list/call/association/paren/part parsers) and a sticky
`saw_newline` flag (set by `skip_whitespace`, cleared when a primary token is
consumed). The implicit-multiplication branch of `parse_expression_prec` breaks
instead of inserting `Times` when `bracket_depth == 0 && saw_newline`. Regression
coverage: `test_parse_newline_separator` in `tests/test_parse.c`.

## CRCTable вҖ” circular вҶ” hyperbolic symmetry fills (2026-07-16)

A systematic audit of the CRC integral table (`src/internal/CRCMathTablesIntegrals.m`)
surfaced a broad set of forms the circular trig rules covered but the hyperbolic
side (and, for reciprocal products, the circular side too) did not. Root causes
and fixes, all verified by differentiate-back to the integrand:

- **`Sinh^m Cosh^n` products** (`Integrate[Sinh[x]^4 Cosh[x]^3, x, Method -> "CRCTable"]`
  returned unevaluated). The Formula 547 reduction bottomed out on single-`Cosh`
  / single-`Sinh` factors that had no base rule. Added the `Sinh Cosh`,
  `Sinh Cosh^m`, `Sinh^m Cosh` bases and generalised Formula 547 to argument
  `a x`.

- **Canonical-head reciprocal products.** Mathilda canonicalises `1/Sin -> Csc`,
  `1/Cos -> Sec`, `1/Sinh -> Csch`, `1/Cosh -> Sech`, so the table's
  `1/(Sin^m Cos^n)` and `1/(Sinh^m Cosh^n)` rules never matched an evaluated
  integrand вҖ” `Csc[x]^4 Sec[x]^3` failed in **both** circular and hyperbolic.
  Added head-keyed reductions on the `Csc/Sec` and `Csch/Sech` heads. The
  recursive term is `+` for circular (`1 = Sin^2 + Cos^2`) and `вҲ’` for
  hyperbolic (`1 = Cosh^2 вҲ’ Sinh^2`).

- **Argument generality (`a x`).** The single-function hyperbolic bases
  (`Sinh`, `Cosh`, `Tanh`, `Coth`, `Sech`, `Csch`), the squares, and
  `Sech Tanh` / `Csch Coth` bound a bare `x`, so every `a != 1` reduction that
  reached a base (e.g. `Sech[2 x]^3`) failed to close. Generalised to `a_. x_`.

- **`E^(a x) Sinh^n` / `E^(a x) Cosh^n`.** Ported the circular `E^(a x) Sin^n` /
  `Cos^n` reductions. The hyperbolic denominator is `a^2 вҲ’ n^2 b^2` (vs the
  circular `a^2 + n^2 b^2`, never zero), which **vanishes at resonance**
  `a = n b` вҖ” e.g. `E^x Cosh[x]` (`a = b = 1`), where `e^x = cosh x + sinh x`
  forces a secular `x/2` term. The reductions carry an `a^2 =!= n^2 b^2` guard
  and resonant `a^2 === b^2` base cases (`e^(2 a x)/(4 a) Вұ x/2`). The `Cosh`
  recursion also carries a `вҲ’` on its integral term where `Sinh` carries `+`
  (the `cosh^2 = 1 + sinh^2` vs `sinh^2 = cosh^2 вҲ’ 1` sign). The exotic
  intermediate resonance `a = n b, n > 1` (e.g. `E^(2x) Cosh[x]^2`) is left to
  the general cascade.

- **Mixed tangent/secant powers** `Tan^m Sec^n`, `Cot^m Csc^n` (circular) and
  `Tanh^m Sech^n`, `Coth^m Csch^n` (hyperbolic). These are the canonical forms
  Mathilda folds the Formula 307/308 quotients into (`Cos^2/Sin^3 -> Cot^2 Csc`,
  `Sin^2/Cos^3 -> Sec Tan^2`), so those quotient rules never matched either. The
  reductions strip the `Tan`/`Cot` power two at a time down to the single
  `Sec^n`/`Csc^n` rules, with the `Tan Sec^n` / `Cot Csc^n` bases (an optional
  exponent covers the single-`Sec`/`Csc` case, i.e. `Sec Tan` and `Csc Cot` вҖ”
  which the circular CRC tables omit though the hyperbolic `Sech Tanh` /
  `Csch Coth` carry them). Recursive-term sign is `вҲ’` for circular
  (`Sec^2 = 1 + Tan^2`) and `+` for hyperbolic (`Sech^2 = 1 вҲ’ Tanh^2`).

- **Removed** the truncated, RHS-less Formula 528 statement
  (`E^(a x) Cos^m Sin^n`), which was dead syntax that never fired.

Not table forms (correctly still unresolved under `Method -> "CRCTable"`, but
handled by the default cascade via Risch): `E^(a x)` times reciprocal trig
powers such as `E^x Sech[x]^3`, `E^x Csch[x]^3` (and the circular `E^x Sec[x]^3`).

- **Polynomial Г— hyperbolic** (`x^n Sinh/Cosh` generalised from bare `x` to
  `a x`; `x Sinh^m` / `x Cosh^m` for Formulas 381-386; `x Csch^n` / `x Sech^n`
  = `x/Sinh^n`, `x/Cosh^n` for Formulas 410-413, keyed on the canonical
  `Csch`/`Sech` heads вҖ” even `n` closes on the `x Csch^2` / `x Sech^2` bases,
  odd `n` bottoms out on the non-elementary `x Csch` / `x Sech` and is left
  unresolved as the circular `x/Sin`, `x/Cos` are). The parallel circular
  `x Csc^n` / `x Sec^n` (dead for the same canonicalisation reason) were
  re-keyed too. `Sinh[a x]/x^m -> SinhIntegral` is intentionally **not** added:

## Hermite reduction over C(x)[t] вҖ” infinite-loop fix on irreducible-quadratic poles (2026-07-14)

indefinitely on normal poles whose squarefree factor is an **irreducible

- New `rf_normfrac` = `Cancel[Together[В·]]`; the division / reduction loops now

## `Sum[]` cascade вҖ” all pre-existing memory leaks fixed (2026-07-13)

Every infinite `Sum` that flowed through the sub-algorithm cascade leaked a
handful of small `Expr` nodes (14вҖ“54 blocks per input, per `SUM_LEAKS.md`),
even when it produced the correct closed form. Root-caused and eliminated; all
five recorded reproducers and every stage now report **0 leaks / 0 bytes**
under macOS `leaks --atExit`.

**Two distinct ownership bugs, both in the stages that run and bail before the
closing stage:**

1. **`evaluate(expr_copy(f))` in 7 stages** вҖ” `evaluate()` *borrows* its
   argument (it internally does `current = expr_copy(e)` and never frees `e`),
   so the anonymous `expr_copy(f)` reference was never released, leaking the
   canonicalised summand tree. Fixed to the correct borrow-then-free idiom
   (`fc = expr_copy(f); fn = evaluate(fc); expr_free(fc);`) in
   `sum_alternating.c`, `sum_trigonometric.c`, `sum_dirichlet.c`, `sum_euler.c`,
   `sum_euler_nonlinear.c`, `sum_geometric.c`, and `sum_zetaseries.c`. `Sum\`Gosper`
   was already clean because it consistently frees the evaluate input.

2. **Redundant `expr_copy(base)` in `Sum\`Rational`** вҖ” `sr_term_contribution`
   uses its `base` argument only through copies (`sr_pdeg`/`sum_subst`), so it
   never needed to own it, yet the sole call site passed `expr_copy(base)` and
   the callee never freed it. Dropped the copy so `base` is borrowed from the
   `Apart` result вҖ” matching the already-clean sibling `alt_term_contribution`.

No behavioural change: `sum_tests` (64) and `nsum_tests` still pass, and the
closed forms are unchanged (`Sum[1/k^2]вҶ’Pi^2/6`, `Sum[1/(k(k+1))]вҶ’1`,
`Sum[(-1)^(k+1)/k]вҶ’Log[2]`, `Sum[1/(k^2+1)]вҶ’(Pi Coth[Pi]-1)/2`, вҖҰ).

first `get_attributes`, eagerly in `attr_init` for table symbols), so every call is now an

  core Protect/Unprotect/ClearAll) need no change.
- Measured on `tests/bench_eval` (Apple M-series): pure-dispatch workload

- Files: `src/symtab.h`, `src/symtab.c`, `src/attr.c`. Struct layout changed вҶ’ full rebuild

## Evaluator/symtab performance вҖ” Phase 2: unified interner + symbol table

The string interner (8191-bucket table of `InternEntry`) and the symbol table
(65535-bucket table of `SymEntry`вҶ’`SymbolDef`) are merged into a single 65535-bucket table
of self-chaining `SymbolDef` nodes. A symbol's name is interned and its definition looked up
through **one** hash of **one** table instead of hashing the name twice across two.

- `SymbolDef` now owns its canonical name string and carries `is_system` (migrated from
  `InternEntry`), a `has_def` materialization flag, and a `next` bucket-chain pointer;
  `SymEntry` is deleted. The `sym_intern.h` API (`intern_symbol`, `intern_is_system`,
  `intern_mark_all_system`, `intern_clear`) is now implemented in `symtab.c` over the unified
  table; `sym_intern.c` is an empty stub.
- `has_def` preserves observable semantics bit-for-bit: `intern_symbol` creates an

## Together вҖ” combine algebraic-constant fractions with non-unit coefficients (2026-07-15)

Fixed a `Together`/`Cancel` deficiency where a sum of fractions over a
constant-radical extension (e.g. `Sqrt[5]`) was left completely uncombined
whenever a polynomial variable carried a coefficient other than 1:

```
Together[1/(2 x - Sqrt[5]) - 1/(2 x + Sqrt[5])]   (* was: unchanged *)
                                              -> (2 Sqrt[5])/(4 x^2 - 5)
```

Root cause was in `exact_poly_div` (`src/poly/poly.c`): the coefficient-level
base case (`var_count == 0`) only returned an exact quotient when **both**
operands were rational/Gaussian-rational, so the leading-coefficient recursion
of the LCM-denominator division `(4x^2 - 5) / (2x Вұ Sqrt[5])` refused to divide
the algebraic coefficient `-2 Sqrt[5]` by the rational `2` and returned NULL вҖ”
`together_recursive`'s strict-quotient combine then read that as "not exactly
divisible" and left the whole sum uncombined. The coefficient-1 case slipped
through only because the divisor was monic.

The fix keys the decision on the **divisor**: division by a nonzero rational (or
Gaussian-rational) scalar is exact in any Q(i)-algebra вҖ” such a `B` is a field
unit, so `A/B = AВ·BвҒ»В№` lands back in the working ring for every `A`, including
algebraic atoms like `Sqrt[2]` or a whole `Q[Sqrt2, Sqrt3, вҖҰ]` coefficient. A
**non-field** divisor (`Sqrt[2]`, a symbolic radical) still returns NULL, so the
case-13 multivariate-GCD termination guard is unaffected. This also lets the
originally-reported mixed exponential/radical input combine (`Sqrt[5]` cancels,
leaving a clean fraction over `Q` in the `2^x` generator):

```
Together[4 Sqrt[5] 2^x (1/(-10 - 10 Sqrt[5] + 20 2^x)
                        - 1/(-10 + 10 Sqrt[5] + 20 2^x))]
    -> 2^x/(2^(2x) - 2^x - 1)          (* equals WL's factored form *)
```

Regression tests in `tests/test_rat.c` (`test_together_algebraic_generator`).

## RischTranscendental: trig/inverse-trig of a logarithm (transcendental power kernels)

`Integrate[f, x, Method -> "RischTranscendental"]` now integrates elementary
integrands whose exponential kernels are hidden as *general powers* `b^e`
(base `b вү  e`, transcendental exponent). These arise whenever a circular or
hyperbolic function of a logarithm is exponentialized: `TrigToExp` turns
`Cos[x Log x]` into `(x^(I x) + x^(-I x))/2`, and the evaluator canonicalizes
`E^(c Log b) вҶ’ b^c` (power.c `simplify_exp_log`), so the kernels
`E^(ВұI x Log x)` are *stored* as `x^(ВұI x)` вҖ” invisible to every exponential
recognizer, all keyed to `Exp` / `Power[E, В·]`. The integrand stranded
unintegrated.

```
Integrate[3 x^2 Cos[x Log[x]] - x^3 Sin[x Log[x]] - x^3 Log[x] Sin[x Log[x]], x,
          Method -> "RischTranscendental"]
    -> x^3 Cos[x Log[x]]                  (* was: unevaluated *)

Integrate[Cos[Log[x]], x, Method -> "RischTranscendental"]
    -> (x/2)(Cos[Log[x]] + Sin[Log[x]])   (* was: unevaluated *)

Integrate[Sin[Log[x]], x, Method -> "RischTranscendental"]  -> (x/2)(Sin[Log x] - Cos[Log x])
Integrate[x^I, x, Method -> "RischTranscendental"]           -> (1/2 - I/2) x^(1+I)
Integrate[x^x (1 + Log[x]), x, Method -> "RischTranscendental"] -> x^x
```

A new structural (non-evaluating) pre-pass `rt_powers_to_exp`
(`src/calculus/integrate_risch_transcendental.c`) re-spells each transcendental
`b^e` as a raw `Power[E, e Log b]` before the tower machinery runs. Because the
rewrite never passes through the evaluator, the `E^(c Log b) вҶ’ b^c` collapse
never fires; the existing collectors/substitution (`rt_collect_exp_exponents`,
`rt_subst_kernels`, which already match `Power[E, В·]` structurally) then see a
genuine hyperexponential monomial, with `Log b` a real logarithmic sub-kernel
(`D[Log x] = 1/x`) вҖ” unlike the constant-base debasing, whose opaque `L_p`
stands for a *constant* `Log p`. Back-substituting the tower kernels lets the
evaluator re-collapse `E^(e Log b) вҶ’ b^e`, rendering the antiderivative in the
original power form (then `ExpToTrig`/realify recover the real trig closed form).

Critically, the exponent is **split** on rewrite: the evaluator merges a
polynomial coefficient into the exponent (`x^2 x^x вҶ’ x^(2+x)`,
`x^2 x^(I x) вҶ’ x^(2+I x)`), which would read as a kernel independent of
`x^(3+x)` / `x^(3+I x)` (exponent ratio non-constant) and defeat the
commensurability reduction. `rt_powers_to_exp` peels the rational-constant
additive terms back into an algebraic power `b^c` (base field), keeping only the
transcendental part as the shared primitive kernel `x^x` / `x^(I x)`.

Genuinely non-elementary Log-in-argument integrands still decline via the field
decision (`Cos[x Log x]`, `Sin[Log x] Log[Log x]`, `x^x`). Every branch remains
behind the exact tower diff-back gate, so a mis-reduction can only decline,
never emit a wrong closed form. Regression coverage in
`tests/test_integrate_risch_transcendental.c` (`test_circular_trig_integration`).

## ElementaryIntegralQ вҖ” decides the trig class + algebraic-scope soundness guard (2026-07-16)

`Risch\`ElementaryIntegralQ[f, x]` is now a decision procedure for the
**trigonometric / inverse-trigonometric** class, and a latent unsoundness on
algebraic-of-x integrands is closed.

- **Trig `False` side.** The field decision (`rt_decide_field`) previously built
  its tower from the raw `f` вҖ” it never ran the integrator's trig front-end вҖ” so
  a bare `Sin`/`Cos`/`Tan` kernel produced no Risch tower and the verdict fell to
  `undec`. It now mirrors `rt_trig_frontend`: `TrigToExp` exponentializes the trig
  kernels (and `rt_powers_to_exp` re-exposes any collapsed `E^(c Log b)` general
  powers), so the decision routes through the *same* Gaussian exponential tower
  the True side uses and reads the residue / Risch-DE certificate off it. The
  classic trig special-function integrals now decide **`False`**:

  ```
  Risch`ElementaryIntegralQ[Sin[x]/x, x]      -> False   (* SinIntegral;  was undec *)
  Risch`ElementaryIntegralQ[Sin[x]/x^2, x]    -> False   (* was undec *)
  Risch`ElementaryIntegralQ[Sin[x^2], x]      -> False   (* FresnelS;     was undec *)
  Risch`ElementaryIntegralQ[Cos[E^x], x]      -> False   (* CosIntegral;  was undec *)
  Risch`ElementaryIntegralQ[Cos[x Log[x]], x] -> False   (* was undec *)
  ```

  Sound because `rt_decide` checks the True side first, so an exhibited elementary
  form always dominates; the field path runs only when the integrator found
  nothing, and its `False` fires solely behind an exact certificate.

- **Algebraic-of-x scope guard (soundness).** Exponentializing a trig/exp of a
  *radical* (`Cos[вҲҡx] вҶ’ E^(ВұIвҲҡx)`) would let the tower builder spuriously accept
  the algebraic `вҲҡx` in an exponent and emit a false `False` вҖ” even though
  `вҲ«Cos(вҲҡx)` is elementary via the algebraic substitution `u=вҲҡx` the
  transcendental algorithm does not perform. `rt_has_algebraic_of_x` now scans the
  original integrand for a non-integer-rational power of an x-dependent base (or a
  `Surd`/`Root` of x) and declines `rt_decide_field` to `UNKNOWN`:

  ```
  Risch`ElementaryIntegralQ[Cos[Sqrt[x]], x]   -> undec  (* elementary via u=Sqrt[x] *)
  Risch`ElementaryIntegralQ[Sin[Sqrt[x]], x]   -> undec
  Risch`ElementaryIntegralQ[Sin[x^(1/3)], x]   -> undec
  Risch`ElementaryIntegralQ[E^Sqrt[x], x]      -> undec  (* fixes a PRE-EXISTING false False *)
  ```

  Mixed algebraic-transcendental Risch (Bronstein's algebraic case) is not
  implemented, so this class is correctly out of scope rather than misdecided.
  `x^x = E^(x Log x)` and `x^Sqrt[2] = E^(Sqrt[2] Log x)` are **not** flagged
  (their exponents are transcendental, not `Rational`-headed).

- Regression coverage in `tests/test_risch_elementaryq.c` (`test_false_trig_frontend`,
  plus trig-True and algebraic-`undec` cases). No regressions across the Risch,
  transcendental-Risch, RischMacsyma, dispatch, DerivativeDivides, Goursat,
  Jeffrey, and unknown integrate suites. Valgrind totals identical to the
  startup baseline (no new leaks).

## Fix: `Integrate` `Method -> "RischTranscendental"` hang on trig-polynomial integrands

`Integrate[x^7 Cos[x] Sin[x]^5, x, Method -> "RischTranscendental"]` (and the
smaller `x Cos[x] Sin[x]^5`) never returned.

- **Root cause.** The trig front-end integrates the exponentialized form, verifies
  it, and reconstructs a real closed form in `rt_realify` (`risch_trig_frontend.c`).
  For a trig-POLYNOMIAL integrand `вҲ« x^n В· trig-poly` the reconstructed real part is
  already the clean multiple-angle antiderivative
  (e.g. `(1/1152)(-90 x Cos[2x] + 36 x Cos[4x] - 6 x Cos[6x] + 45 Sin[2x] - 9 Sin[4x] + Sin[6x])`),
  but `rt_realify` handed it to full `Simplify` purely to beautify it. `Simplify`'s
  transform search `Factor`s the `TrigExpand` image, and the multivariate factorizer
  is pathologically slow (~6 s per term) over the OVERLAPPING generators
  `{x, Cos[x], Sin[x]}` вҖ” `Cos[x]`/`Sin[x]` themselves contain `x`. With seven
  exponential kernels the total blew up unboundedly.

- **Fix.** `rt_realify` now takes a fast path for a trig-polynomial real form
  (free of `Log`/`Arc*` heads and of denominators/radicals): normalize with the
  cheap, linear `TrigReduce` (product-to-sum) instead of `Simplify`, and return it
  once the existing exact diff-back gate (`Together[TrigToExp[D вҲ’ f]]`, which reduces
  to 0 immediately for a denominator-free trig polynomial) certifies it. Rational-trig
  answers (`Sec[x]`, `1/(2+Cos[x])`, вҖҰ) carry `Log`/`ArcTan`, are excluded from the
  fast path, and go through `Simplify` unchanged. Correctness is unaffected вҖ” the same
  diff-back gate certifies the result; only the beautification path changed.

- `x^7 Cos[x] Sin[x]^5` now returns a correct multiple-angle antiderivative
  (diff-back `= 0`); `x Cos[x] Sin[x]^5` and `x^2 Cos[x] Sin[x]^3` return in ~0.15 s.
  Regression cases added to `tests/test_integrate_risch_transcendental.c`
  (`test_reported_bug_fixes`). No regressions across the transcendental-Risch,
  risch_field, inverse-trig-Simplify, and trigrat suites.

## Integration вҖ” Cherry ExpIntegralEi engine (rational exponential integral, C1) (2026-07-16)

First increment of the Cherry special-function extension (see `CHERRY_PLAN.md`):
a genuine base-field **`ExpIntegralEi`** engine for the rational exponential
integral `INT g E^f dx` with `g, f` rational in `x` (G. W. Cherry, *An Analysis
of the Rational Exponential Integral*, 1989). New `src/calculus/cherry_ei.c`
(`rt_cherry_ei`), registered as an `ExpIntegralEi` form in the
`RtSpecialForm` registry (`risch_special.c`) behind the existing narrow
`rt_try_ei` fast path.

- **Argument generation.** The `ei` arguments `f + alpha_i` come from the
  resultant `Res_x(g1, p + alpha q)` (P1: `f = p/q` reduced/monic, `g1` = the
  `q`-coprime part of `den(g)`) together with the single `q`-side term `f`
  itself (P2). `y` and the constants `c_i` are solved as **one linear system
  over the constants** from the rational matching identity
  `g = y' + f' y + sum_i c_i f'/(f + alpha_i)` (the exponential kernel divides
  out) via `SolveAlways` вҖ” the 1989 undetermined-coefficient solve, **not**
  Risch's differential equation. Emits
  `y E^f + sum_i c_i E^(-alpha_i) ExpIntegralEi[f + alpha_i]`.
- **Closes** (all diff-back verified, previously returned unevaluated
  `nonelem`): `Integrate[E^(1/x),x] = x E^(1/x) - ExpIntegralEi[1/x]` (Cherry
  Ex 5.1); `Integrate[E^x/(x+1)^2,x] = ExpIntegralEi[x+1]/E - E^x/(x+1)` (d8);
  `Integrate[(x^2+3)E^x/(x^2+3x+2),x] = E^x - 7 ExpIntegralEi[x+2]/E^2 +
  4 ExpIntegralEi[x+1]/E` (d11, multi-term via resultant roots `alpha = 1,2`);
  and nonlinear exponents `Integrate[E^(x^2)/x,x] = ExpIntegralEi[x^2]/2`.
- **Sound decline.** Algebraic `ei`-argument constants (`E^x/(x^2-2)` вҶ’ `sqrt2`,
  `E^x/(x^2+1)` вҶ’ `ВұI`) need the algebraically-closed-constant layer (a later
  phase); the engine declines cleanly (leaves the integral unevaluated) rather
  than emit a partial or wrong form. Every emitted answer passes the exact
  diff-back gate `rt_verify_antideriv`, and a driver-side guard rejects any
  candidate that still hides an unevaluated `Integrate` head (so the FTC rule
  cannot rubber-stamp an open integral). These integrals remain non-elementary:
  `ElementaryIntegralQ` is unchanged (still `False`).
- **Higher-multiplicity poles.** The elementary part `y = Y/s` uses the *full*
  denominator `den(g) q` (not its radical): a pole of multiplicity `m` in `g`
  leaves a pole of multiplicity `m-1` in `y` (the ei/log-derivative term removes
  one power), so `den(y)` is generally not squarefree вҖ” e.g.
  `Integrate[E^x/(x+1)^3,x] = (1/2) ExpIntegralEi[x+1]/E - E^x (x+2)/(2(x+1)^2)`.
  Repeated and multi-factor denominators (`E^x/(x+1)^3`, `E^x/x^4`,
  `E^x/((x+1)^2(x+2)^2)`, `E^(x^2)/x^3`) now close.
- New tests `tests/test_cherry_ei.c` (`cherry_ei_tests`), including an extensive
  stress battery (~55 cases across linear / reciprocal / polynomial exponents,
  repeated & multi-pole denominators, ei-cancels-to-elementary, and algebraic
  declines). Two negative pins in `tests/test_integrate_risch_transcendental.c`
  that codified the previous incapability (`E^x/(x+2)^2`, `E^x/(x-3)^2`,
  `E^(1/x)`, `E^x/x^2` expected unevaluated) updated to assert the new `ei`
  closed forms. No regressions across the transcendental-Risch, elementaryq,
  dispatch, and RischNorman suites.

## Integration вҖ” Cherry ExpIntegralEi: algebraic-constant layer (C2 В§7) (2026-07-16)

The `ExpIntegralEi` engine (`cherry_ei.c`) now admits **real algebraic**
ei-argument constants, closing the rational exponential integral over an
irreducible-quadratic (or higher) denominator whose roots are algebraic вҖ” the
Cherry `C = C-bar` case:

- `Integrate[E^x/(x^2-2),x] = (1/(2 Sqrt[2]))(E^Sqrt[2] ExpIntegralEi[x-Sqrt[2]]
  - E^(-Sqrt[2]) ExpIntegralEi[x+Sqrt[2]])` (Cherry 1989, p.894);
- `E^x/(x^2-3)`, `E^x/(x^2-5)`, `E^x/(x^2-x-1)` (golden ratio), scaled
  `E^(2x)/(x^2-5)`, etc. вҖ” all diff-back verified.

Mechanism: the P1 resultant roots `alpha_i` are no longer restricted to `Q`;
`Solve` returns the algebraic roots and the matching-identity `SolveAlways`
solves the coefficients over the extension they generate (e.g. `Q(Sqrt d)`), with
`e^(-alpha_i)` prefactors carrying the algebraic exponent.

Two robustness measures were required:
- **Reality gate (numeric).** Symbolic `Im[]`/`Element[.,Reals]` do not reduce
  radicals, so a candidate root is admitted only if its *numeric* imaginary part
  is ~0. This only SELECTS candidates вҖ” the exact diff-back still certifies every
  emission вҖ” so a mis-gated root can at worst cause a decline. **Complex**
  ei-argument constants (`E^x/(x^2+1)` -> +-I, d12 `E^x/(x^2+x+1)`) are therefore
  deferred (a real conjugate-pair reconstruction is a later refinement); they
  decline cleanly rather than emit a complex `ExpIntegralEi` form the diff-back
  cannot reduce.
- **Rational-pole y-denominator.** The elementary part `y = Y/s` uses `s` = the
  product of the degree-1 (rational-root) factors of `den(g) q` only; an
  algebraic-root factor (e.g. `x^2 - d`) stays out of `y`'s denominator (`y` is
  polynomial there). Besides matching Cherry's structure, this sidesteps a shared
  `Together`/`SolveAlways`-over-`Q(Sqrt d)` stack overflow that fires when an
  algebraic ei pole `x -+ Sqrt d` is reduced against a `y`-denominator carrying
  the same reducible factor `x^2 - d` (observed for `d >= 5`; the pure-ei/
  polynomial-`y` path is unaffected). No `FactorList` builtin exists; the factor
  walk uses `Factor`.

Also in this increment: **higher-multiplicity poles now close** (the `y`
denominator uses full multiplicity вҖ” `E^x/(x+1)^3`, `E^x/x^4`,
`E^x/((x+1)^2(x+2)^2)`), and `tests/test_cherry_ei.c` gained an extensive stress
battery (~60 cases: linear/reciprocal/polynomial exponents, repeated & multi-pole
denominators, real-algebraic constants, ei-cancels-to-elementary, and complex/
cubic declines). No regressions across the transcendental-Risch, elementaryq,
dispatch, and RischNorman suites.

## Integration вҖ” Cherry Erf/Erfi engine (completing-square, C2 В§3) (2026-07-16)

The Cherry rational-exponential engine (`cherry_ei.c`) now emits **error-function**
terms as well as `ExpIntegralEi`, completing Cherry 1989 for the base field: an
integrand `g E^f` whose exponent denominator `q` (in `f = p/q`) is a **perfect
square** `q = s^2` admits up to two `Erfi` terms (Thm 3.2), solved in the SAME
linear system as the `ei` terms.

- **Argument generation (В§3).** The error-function arguments are `u~_j = r_j/s`
  with `r_j^2 = p + beta_j q`. The `beta_j` are found by completing the square:
  candidates are the roots (in `b`) of the discriminant
  `Res_x(p + b q, d/dx(p + b q))` plus `b = 0`, each validated by `PolynomialSqrt`
  and an exact `Expand[r^2 - (p + beta q)] == 0` certificate. Because `s = Sqrt[q]`
  is a **polynomial**, `u~ = r/s` is rational, so the erf contribution
  `k_j (2/Sqrt[Pi]) (r_j/s)'` slots straight into the rational matching identity.
- **Closes** (all diff-back verified): Ex 5.2
  `Integrate[(1/x+1/x^2) E^(1/x^2),x] = -1/2 ExpIntegralEi[1/x^2] - (Sqrt[Pi]/2) Erfi[1/x]`;
  Ex 5.4 (symbolic parameter)
  `Integrate[E^((x^4+a)/x^2),x] = (Sqrt[Pi]/4)(E^(-2Sqrt[a]) Erfi[(x^2+Sqrt[a])/x]
  + E^(2Sqrt[a]) Erfi[(x^2-Sqrt[a])/x])`; the pure `E^(1/x^2)`; and concrete
  two-erf cases `E^((x^4+4)/x^2)` etc. (Cherry's `erf(u) = INT u' e^(u^2) =
  (Sqrt[Pi]/2) Erfi(u)`, so answers carry `Sqrt[Pi]` against Mathilda's `Erfi`.)
- **Symbolic-parameter fix.** The coefficient solve switched from `SolveAlways`
  (which also tries to solve for a symbolic PARAMETER like the `a` in Ex 5.4) to
  `Solve` over the ansatz unknowns explicitly (`CoefficientList` вҶ’ `Thread[.==0]`
  вҶ’ `Solve[eqs, {unknowns}]`). This keeps integrand parameters as parameters; all
  earlier ei/algebraic cases are unaffected.
- **Decline-safety.** A non-perfect-square `q` (e.g. `q = x^3` in
  `E^((x^6+8)/x^3)`) contributes 0 erf terms; complex `beta` (numeric imaginary
  part) is deferred by the shared real-admissibility gate. New tests in
  `tests/test_cherry_ei.c` (`test_cherry_erf`, `test_cherry_erf_symbolic`). No
  regressions; zero leaks over baseline.

## Integration вҖ” Cherry C2 stress test + known gaps (2026-07-16)

Extensive stress battery added to `tests/test_cherry_ei.c` (`test_stress_c2`)
covering real-algebraic ei over Q(sqrt d) (d = 2,3,5,6,7,10; golden ratio; non-monic
and improper cofactors; scaled/negated exponents) and erf (multi-term, symbolic
parameter b). Two SHARED-function limitations surfaced (NOT Cherry logic; both
decline cleanly вҖ” never a wrong form вҖ” and are pinned as declines so a future fix
trips the test):

1. **`Together`/`Solve` over `Q(sqrt d)` does not reduce conjugate linear
   factors** `(x - 1 - sqrt2)(x - 1 + sqrt2)` back to `x^2 - 2 x - 1`. For an
   ei-pole quadratic with irrational roots and a non-zero linear term (e.g.
   `E^x/(x^2 - 2 x - 1)`, or the mixed `E^x/((x^2-2)(x+1))`), the common
   denominator over-inflates and the linear system is overdetermined -> decline.
   (The `x^2 - d` and golden-ratio forms happen to reduce and DO close.)
2. **`PolynomialSqrt` fails on NUMERIC radical coefficients** вҖ” `PolynomialSqrt[
   x^4 + 2 Sqrt[2] x^2 + 2]` returns `$Failed` although it equals `(x^2+Sqrt[2])^2`
   (and `PolynomialSqrt[x^4 + 2 Sqrt[a] x^2 + a]` with a symbolic succeeds). So the
   erf case `E^((x^4+2)/x^2)` (beta = +-2 Sqrt[2]) declines while the symbolic and
   rational-beta cases close.

Both are recorded in `TOGETHER_ALGEBRAIC_OVERFLOW.md` (algebraic-normaliser area).

## Integration вҖ” Cherry LogIntegral engine (single-log multi-li, C3) (2026-07-16)

First increment of the Cherry logarithmic-integral engine (Cherry 1986): a
single-log tower `theta = Log[w]` (w a polynomial in x) with li arguments that are
POWERS `w^k`. New `src/calculus/cherry_li.c` (`rt_cherry_li`), registered as a
`LogIntegral` form (`risch_special.c`) behind the narrow `rt_try_li` fast path.
Generalises `rt_try_li` from a single `li` term to a SUM.

- **Tower matching.** The answer `v(x, theta) + Sum_k d_k LogIntegral[w^k]` is
  matched treating `theta` as an independent variable вҖ” `Log[w^k] = k theta` by
  construction, so `D[LogIntegral[w^k]] = w^(k-1) w'/theta` вҖ” giving the rational
  identity `gamma = D_tower[v] + Sum_k d_k w^(k-1) w'/theta`
  (`D_tower = d/dx + (w'/w) d/dtheta`), solved by `SolveAlways` over `{theta, x}`.
  The exact tower identity IS the certificate; the emission is additionally
  PowerExpand-diff-back verified (a plain Simplify cannot reduce
  `Log[w^k] = k Log[w]`, a branch-cut subtlety).
- **Closes** (PowerExpand + numeric diff-back verified): Cherry Ex 1.1 / d1
  `INT x/Log[x]^2 = 2 li(x^2) - x^2/Log[x]`; d3
  `INT x^2/Log[x+1] = li((x+1)^3) - 2 li((x+1)^2) + li(x+1)`; Ex 5.1
  `INT x^3/Log[x^2-1] = (1/2) li(x^2-1) + (1/2) li((x^2-1)^2)` (solved directly
  via the single generator `w = x^2 - 1`, without the paper's 2-log tower); plus a
  broad range (`x^3/Log[x^2]`, `x^4/Log[x+2]`, `1/Log[2x]`, `2/Log[x]^3`, ...).
- **Decline-safety.** The transcendental-constant rescaling (d2:
  `1/(Log[x]+3) -> e^-3 li(e^3 x)`), a non-polynomial log argument
  (`x/Log[Sin[x]]`), and reducible `w` needing a genuine product Sigma-decomposition
  (`x^3/Log[x^3-x]`) decline cleanly. New tests `tests/test_cherry_li.c`
  (`cherry_li_tests`). No regressions; zero leaks over baseline.

## Integration вҖ” Cherry LogIntegral: transcendental-constant rescaling (2026-07-16)

The li engine (`cherry_li.c`) now handles the transcendental-constant RESCALING:
a **constant nonzero root** `rho` of the theta-denominator (theta = Log[w])
contributes a rescaled logarithmic integral, since `Log[w] - rho = Log[e^-rho w]`,
so a simple pole `1/(theta - rho)` integrates to `e^rho li(e^-rho w)`.

- Mechanism: `Solve[den_theta == 0, theta]` -> keep constant nonzero roots
  `rho_i`; add basis terms `k_i w'/(theta - rho_i)` to the tower match and answer
  terms `k_i e^rho_i LogIntegral[e^(-rho_i) w]`. Solved in the same SolveAlways
  over `{theta, x}` as the power-family li terms.
- **Closes** (PowerExpand + numeric diff-back verified): Cherry d2
  `Integrate[1/(Log[x]+3),x] = LogIntegral[E^3 x]/E^3`; d4
  `Integrate[(Log[x]^2+3)/(Log[x]^2+3 Log[x]+2),x] = x + 4 LogIntegral[E x]/E
  - 7 LogIntegral[E^2 x]/E^2` (a rational function of Log[x], Cherry's Thm 5.5
  family, with the polynomial part giving the elementary `x`). Also
  `1/(Log[x]+1)`, `1/((Log[x]+1)(Log[x]+2))`, etc.
- Tests in `tests/test_cherry_li.c` (`test_cherry_li_rescale`). No regressions;
  zero leaks over baseline.

## Integration вҖ” Cherry ExpIntegralEi: complex-quadratic constants (2026-07-16)

The ei engine (`cherry_ei.c`) now closes a single irreducible-quadratic
denominator with COMPLEX conjugate roots (f a polynomial, q = 1), emitting a
complex-conjugate pair of `ExpIntegralEi` terms:
`Integrate[E^x/(x^2+1),x] = (I/2)(ExpIntegralEi[I+x]/E^I - E^I ExpIntegralEi[x-I])`
(diff-back 0, fast over Q(i)); also `E^x/(x^2+9)`, `E^(2x)/(x^2+1)`,
`E^x/(x^2+2x+2)`.

- The candidate reality gate changed from a numeric `Im ~ 0` test to a
  **degree gate**: admit a constant iff `MinimalPolynomial` has degree <= 2 (real
  OR complex quadratic), symbolic constants admitted (MinimalPolynomial declines).
  Degree >= 3 (`x^4-2`, cube roots) is deferred вҖ” the complex diff-back over a
  higher-degree extension blows up.
- Additionally, a complex root is admitted (for ei) ONLY as the sole conjugate
  pair: `q = 1` (no P2 term) and `g1` an irreducible quadratic. Mixing a complex
  pair with any other pole (a P2/reciprocal term, a rational factor) blows up
  `Together` over Q(i), so those decline cleanly (`E^(1/x)/(x^2+1)`,
  `E^x/((x^2+1)(x-1))`). Denominators over Q(i sqrt3) (d12 `x^2+x+1`) also decline
  (the C2 G1 `Together`-non-reduction gap). Erf keeps real-only beta.
- No regressions; zero leaks over baseline; every complex case is fast (no hangs).

## Integration вҖ” Fresnel integrals (Gaussian-phase trig, C4) (2026-07-16)

square вҖ” the trigonometric sibling of the `K E^(a x^2+b x+c) -> Erf` recognizer.

## Integration вҖ” Cherry dilogarithm engine (degree-2 Sigma-decomposition) (2026-07-16)

Generalises the single-term `rt_try_dilog` recognizer (`K Log[1+p x]/x ->
-K PolyLog[2,-p x]`) to the full `Log-Log + PolyLog[2]` answer form. New
`src/calculus/cherry_dilog.c` (`rt_cherry_dilog`), registered as a `PolyLog` form
(`risch_special.c`) behind the fast path.

- **Tower matching.** The answer `Sum c_ij Log[f_i] Log[f_j] + Sum d_k

  independent variable; `D[PolyLog[2,g]] = -Log[1-g] g'/g` rationalises in the

## Fix: `Expand[expr, patt]` no longer distributes pattern-free factors (2026-07-17)

The two-argument form of `Expand` incorrectly distributed *every* `Plus`
factor of a product, even those free of the pattern. `Expand[(a+b)(x[1]+x[2])^2,
x[_]]` returned the fully expanded `a x[1]^2 + b x[1]^2 + ...` instead of keeping
`(a+b)` as an unexpanded coefficient.

- **Root cause.** The `Times` branch of `expr_expand_patt` (`src/expand.c`)
  expanded each factor and then `multiply_all` distributed all `Plus` factors
  together, ignoring which factors were free of `patt`.
- **Fix.** The `Times` branch now partitions factors into pattern-free (carried
  as an atomic, never-distributed coefficient) and pattern-containing (expanded
  and multiplied out). The free coefficient is distributed over each summand of
  the expanded active part without being expanded itself. With a `NULL` pattern
  every factor is active, so single-argument `Expand` is unchanged.
- `Expand[(a+b)(x[1]+x[2])^2, x[_]]` now gives
  `(a+b) x[1]^2 + 2 (a+b) x[1] x[2] + (a+b) x[2]^2`. New regression cases in
  `tests/test_expand.c`; no regressions.
